Roadmap · Phase-gated development · No production date promised

Build the control system before trusting automation.

IntelliBot’s roadmap is intentionally evidence-gated. The product moves from governance, schema readiness, and local foundation into market data, strategy proof, sandbox simulation, live-execution hardening, cockpit UI, advisory context, and only later commercialization or compliance review.

Current phasePhase 0 · governance, planning, architecture, and migration-readiness controls
Current public statusNo public product release, beta enrollment, or investment offering
Release principleEvidence before live capital · sandbox before production arming
Timing posturePhase completion is quality-gated, not calendar-promised
Roadmap standard

This is a gated engineering roadmap, not a launch hype calendar.

IntelliBot should not move forward because a page says it will. It moves forward only when the relevant phase evidence, product controls, security posture, risk validation, and approval gates are satisfied.

The product direction is ambitious: an advanced local trading cockpit with risk-state persistence, proof-gated strategies, non-custodial exchange integration, structural entries, order-safety hardening, and advisory intelligence. That ambition requires sequencing.

Non-negotiable release controls
  • No live-capital path before backtesting and sandbox validation.
  • No AI-autopilot authority; AI remains advisory context only.
  • No public fundraising language unless a later financing pathway is approved.
  • No beta onboarding until product, policy, security, and consent controls are ready.
  • No schema or audit-readiness language may be represented as regulatory certification.
Development map

Phase-gated build sequence

The roadmap below reflects the current public-safe product direction. It describes planned development sequencing and control objectives, not currently released functionality.

0
Current · Phase 0

Governance, architecture, and clean foundation

Lock the project constitution, TPMS ledger, Codex Plan-mode instructions, phase gatekeeper rules, risk-first product boundaries, strategy taxonomy, UI architecture, commercialization controls, and public website perimeter.

  • STATUS.md remains the authoritative ticket ledger.
  • Current ticket range extends through TT-169.
  • Schema and Phase 1 migration-readiness blockers are documented before implementation begins.
  • Website and marketing copy remain supporting artifacts and cannot override product authority.
1
Phase 1

Local foundation, credentials, persistence, and diagnostics

Establish the Windows-first local application foundation: settings, Windows-safe paths, encrypted local credential storage, SQLite schema hardening, operational logging, Coinbase diagnostics, setup gating, account selection, sandbox ledger, and persistence contracts.

  • Master-password encrypted local credential envelope and no-recovery reset lifecycle.
  • SQLite WAL/busy-timeout profile and schema migrations.
  • Active-trade management state, one-active-trade database enforcement, drawdown state, exit settings, synthetic candle markers, and strategy proof placeholder persistence.
  • Diagnostics and account setup without withdrawal-permission dependency.
2
Phase 2

Market data, indicators, screener, strategy contracts, and watchdogs

Build the signal spine: Coinbase product discovery, candle normalization, indicator engine, technical screener, candidate ranking, strategy signal contracts, strategy manager, and AccountSafetyWatchdog behavior.

  • Coinbase Spot market universe and USD spot eligibility filters.
  • Handling for native candles and synthetic 3-minute aggregation where required.
  • Strategy families including ORB/scalp/swing foundations and later structural strategy candidates.
  • Account, stale-data, spread, liquidity, volatility, and active-trade conflict blockers.
3
Phase 3

Risk engine, sandbox broker, order lifecycle, and canonical backtester

Prove the trading logic without live capital. Phase 3 centers on RiskManager, sandbox execution, order lifecycle modeling, and the internal event-driven backtester as the canonical proof engine.

  • Modeled fees, spread, slippage, exchange constraints, same-candle arbitration, and no-lookahead safeguards.
  • Strategy promotion gates for backtest proof, scenario analysis, parameter sensitivity, and sample size.
  • Sandbox replay, restart/reconciliation tests, stale-feed failure tests, and audit-linked evidence identity.
  • Readiness gates that explain why a strategy is unproven, disabled, degraded, or ineligible.
4
Phase 4

Live execution hardening after deterministic simulation

Live execution is not the starting point. It is reached only after deterministic simulation, risk controls, account checks, and order-lifecycle behavior are proven against the product boundary.

  • Production arming remains explicit and gated.
  • ExecutionEngine must preserve long-only Coinbase Spot boundaries.
  • Exit handling includes fee-adjusted break-even, Profit Guard planning, compound invalidation, partial-fill stop handling, and staged emergency exit escalation.
  • Kill switch, open-order reconciliation, active-trade conflict resolution, and live-readiness evidence are required.
5
Phase 5

Operator cockpit UI and progressive unlock workflow

Deliver the PyQt5 desktop cockpit after core services exist. The UI model uses seven surfaces: Dashboard, Live Trading, Strategies, Backtesting, Risk Center, Reporting, and Settings.

  • Settings-only first-run lock with progressive navigation unlock.
  • Live Trading owns embedded candidate screener, integrated chart, operational advisory context, guardrails, decision log, active-position lifecycle, and emergency controls.
  • Dashboard remains a command center, not a chart/screener dumping ground.
  • Screener rows require semantic state badges and reason text: Blocked, Watchlist, Qualified, Armed Candidate, or Active Position.
6
Phase 6

Advisory context, alerts, deployment packaging, and operator support

Add advisory market context only after the core trading spine is controlled. News, sentiment, AI summaries, alerts, and deployment packaging must support explanation and risk context without becoming autonomous trading authority.

  • AI/news/sentiment modules are advisory and risk-context systems only.
  • Provider/source configuration belongs in Settings; operational context appears inside Dashboard, Live Trading, Backtesting, Risk Center, and Reporting.
  • High-severity events may create warnings or blockers if the deterministic risk framework supports that behavior.
  • Advisory context cannot open trades, increase risk, or create live eligibility.
7
Phase 7

Commercialization, beta, compliance, and funding-pathway review

Commercial activity comes last. Formation, public policies, beta agreements, telemetry decisions, payment/subscription systems, capital-raising pathway selection, investor communications, and public launch claims require separate later-phase approval and review.

  • Public website policies require entity, data-processor, retention, consent, and counsel-reviewed publication controls before commercial activity.
  • No public investment opportunity is offered through the site.
  • No funding pathway is selected by roadmap discussion alone.
  • Any future securities or crowdfunding path must be separately selected, reviewed, documented, and conducted through the appropriate channel.
Current update focus

Schema and migration readiness before Phase 1

The latest architecture work tightens the persistence model before the first implementation batch. This is engineering readiness, not a regulatory audit or compliance certification.

Active trade lifecycle

Management state persistence

Active trades need durable management state, risk profile at entry, frozen risk constants, and lifecycle evidence so restart and reconciliation behavior can be controlled.

Exposure enforcement

One-active-trade constraint

The planning baseline requires database-level enforcement for the one-active-trade model in addition to application-layer RiskManager and watchdog controls.

Risk continuity

Daily and weekly drawdown state

Loss accounting, UTC anchors, cooldown state, and weekly drawdown behavior must persist across restarts instead of living only in memory.

Exit policy

Profit Guard and invalidation flags

Exit settings such as Profit Guard and compound invalidation behavior must be represented in persistence before trade-management behavior is trusted.

Data provenance

Synthetic candle markers

Cached candle data must distinguish native exchange candles from synthetic aggregation so backtesting and live-parity logic can explain its data source.

Proof status

Strategy proof placeholder

Strategy proof status needs a durable placeholder before later backtesting and sandbox promotion flows can use it as eligibility evidence.

Promotion gates

A strategy does not become live because it is coded

Each strategy must earn promotion through evidence. The planned readiness path is deliberately layered.

GateRequired evidence directionWhat it prevents
Backtest proofDeterministic backtest with fees, spread, slippage, minimum sample size, max drawdown threshold, profit-factor review, win/loss distribution, and scenario analysis.Prevents untested strategies from being presented as live-ready.
Sandbox validationReplay and simulation against venue-like constraints, stale-feed behavior, restart/reconciliation drills, and controlled order-lifecycle behavior.Prevents backtest-only confidence from being mistaken for execution readiness.
Trading readinessAPI health, account selection, no-withdrawal expectation, encrypted credentials, risk profile, equity floor, active-trade conflict status, and kill-switch readiness.Prevents live arming when the account, environment, or safety controls are not ready.
Risk and account safetyRiskManager and AccountSafetyWatchdog approve the trade path, including spread, volatility, drawdown, cooldown, data quality, account state, and active exposure.Prevents strategy signals from becoming orders without governance.
Human understandingDecision cards, blocked-trade explanations, readiness reasons, post-trade forensics, and education-aware UI mode behavior.Prevents opaque automation and helps the user understand restraint as a feature.
What is intentionally not on the roadmap yet
  • No multi-exchange support in the initial active trading scope.
  • No derivatives, futures, perps, swaps, margin, leverage, equities, or ETFs.
  • No copy-trading marketplace or social signal resale model.
  • No hosted multi-user execution platform in the local-alpha scope.
  • No set-it-and-forget-it profit claims.
Timing guidance
