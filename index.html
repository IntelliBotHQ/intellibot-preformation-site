Product thesis

Always-on monitoring should never mean uncontrolled trading.

The planned product is scoped around disciplined automation and transparent safety boundaries rather than trade-frequency claims or performance promises.

Risk-governed

Proof before live

Planned validation gates emphasize backtesting and sandbox evidence before live eligibility.

Local-first

User control

The intended architecture keeps credentials and execution close to the user rather than holding funds.

Scoped

Spot and long-only

Initial direction is Coinbase spot-first, long-only, and one active position at a time by default.

Explainable

AI advisory only

AI is intended to explain context, never authorize trades or override deterministic risk controls.

Dashboard concept. Risk state, readiness, market context, advisory context, and decision logs are presented as the planned command-center direction. All values are illustrative placeholders only.
Illustrative product direction

What these panels are meant to communicate

  • Risk, proof, and readiness should be visible before any trading action.
  • The application is being designed as a multi-surface operator cockpit, not a single black-box signal screen.
  • Every surface has a distinct role: monitor, validate, block, explain, execute, report, or configure.
  • The final interface may look similar in direction, but copy, layout, data fields, and controls remain subject to engineering and review.
